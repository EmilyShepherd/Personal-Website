If you want to get in contact, please use my new email address: emily@emilyshepherd.me.

So what does this mean?

I'm transgender, which means I was born in body that doesn't match my mind - my sex is male, but my gender is female. Now that I have come to terms with this and come out, I ask that people refer to me as "Emily" and use female pronouns (her, she, etc).

What about "Shep"?

Shep has been my nickname for as long as I can remember, and I'm still completely happy with it. My friends have also started to use "Em" as a nickname.

What if I forget?

I might remind you if you accidentally refer to me as "Alex", but please don't take this as I sign that I am upset with you.

Is this "Legal" Yet?

I've put "legal" in quotation marks because I have the right to be referred to with the name / pronouns that are appropriate for me. However, I have not formally changed my name yet, so my passport / birth certificate still state that I am male. For this reason, if you need to use my name in a formal or legal setting (eg, for bank transfers), please continue to use "ALEXANDER M SHEPHERD" for the meantime.
